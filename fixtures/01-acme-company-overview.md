# Acme Corp — Company Overview

**Last updated:** January 2026
**Owner:** Office of the CEO
**Classification:** Internal

## What we do

Acme Corp builds **FlowState**, a cloud-based workflow-automation platform that helps
operations teams design, run, and monitor business processes without writing code. Customers
connect their existing tools (CRMs, data warehouses, ticketing systems, and messaging apps),
then automate hand-offs, approvals, and alerts through a visual builder.

Founded in **2017** and headquartered in **Austin, Texas**, Acme Corp employs roughly **480
people** across product, engineering, sales, and customer success. We are a remote-first
company with hubs in Austin, Denver, and Lisbon.

## Mission

> Give every operations team the power to automate their busywork so they can focus on the work
> that actually moves the business.

## Target customers

Our ideal customer profile (ICP) is the **mid-market to enterprise operations organization**
(500–10,000 employees) in three primary verticals:

1. **Financial services** — reconciliation, compliance workflows, and customer onboarding.
2. **Healthcare & life sciences** — patient intake, claims routing, and audit trails.
3. **Logistics & supply chain** — exception handling, vendor coordination, and SLA tracking.

Buyers are typically **VPs of Operations**, **Directors of Business Systems**, and **RevOps
leaders**. We sell both direct (mid-market and enterprise) and through a self-serve tier for
teams getting started.

## Product lines

- **FlowState Core** — the visual workflow builder and runtime.
- **FlowState Insights** — analytics and SLA dashboards on top of running workflows.
- **FlowState Connect** — the integration layer (200+ prebuilt connectors).

## How we make money

Acme Corp sells annual SaaS subscriptions priced per active workflow seat, with usage-based
add-ons for high-volume automation runs. Average contract value (ACV) for enterprise deals is
approximately **$140,000/year**.
